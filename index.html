Author

Samuel Mac Dowell

I’ve had a rather un-orthodox path towards applying for Illustration. I first did a semester in mechatronic engineering, but I decided rather to pursue a different path with the same goal. That is to combine all these incredibly different fields of knowledge, from engineering and science to arts and design, and find a dialogue between them. Above all the concept of time intrigued me, and Masahiro Kikuno, a Japanese watchmaker who amazed me, from his technics from the manufacturing by hand each watch. A level of mastery of both the mechanical and the arts plus the knowledge to make it. That would be something to aspire to. Artists such as On Kawara show how to express concepts like of time in a more experimental conceptual scenario, that even though not as material as I’d hope, is a steppingstone to any project. More contemporary artists like Olafur Eliasson seem to explore this idea to transform a whole room and swallow the viewer in its piece. My objective would be to use all these ways they used to transmit not just feeling, but knowledge as well, or at least enough wonder that inspire people to want to know how that works.

Work

Materials
Plaster, wood, steel wire, mechanical pendulum clock

Project
The project intends to discuss Time. It is a city, a scale model of sorts, of a skyline suspended over a ticking clock. The plan was to explore the different ways we interact with it, the aspect of the physical reality of the forwarding of. For us time is too much, too little and never enough, and always related to what we are doing, but in a broader sense it is forever and impartial to any event, variable but in the end universal.
